The kit preview is pointing at the wrong card.

When you choose a top-row project type, the detail panel opens below the whole grid — and its little arrow lands under the bottom row. So the preview reads as if it belongs to a card you didn't pick. Four ways to fix the broken connection.

C Compass · UX Designer  ·  design exploration, not production code

DIAGNOSIS What's going wrong

The picker is a 3×2 grid; the selected kit's preview renders full-width, after the entire grid, with a fixed caret near its top-left. Select a top-row card and the caret sits under the bottom-left card — a textbook break of Gestalt proximity & continuity: the eye ties the panel to whatever sits directly above it, not to the highlighted card two rows up.

OPTION A Row-aware inline expansion

The preview opens directly beneath the row of the chosen card, and the caret tracks the selected card's column. Pick a top-row card → it expands under row 1, nudging row 2 down. The arrow can never lie. This is the Linear / Notion “expand-in-place” pattern (Jakob's Law — already familiar).

Click between top-row and bottom-row cards — the panel and caret follow your choice.

Pros

  • Caret & proximity are always honest — the panel is adjacent to its card.
  • Smallest change to the current layout; one column, fits the idea form & onboarding as-is.
  • Familiar accordion behaviour; no new mental model.

Cons / watch-outs

  • Selecting a top-row card shifts the lower row down — animate the insert so it doesn't feel jumpy.
  • Caret-under-column needs the preview to know the selected column (simple math).

📱Mobile: grid collapses to 2 (or 1) columns, preview still opens under the tapped row — behaviour is identical, no special-casing.

OPTION B Master–detail side panel

Cards on the left, a persistent detail panel on the right that swaps content as you select. No vertical caret to mislead anyone; the tie is carried by the highlight plus the panel header echoing the chosen card's icon & name. Zero layout shift, and it scales gracefully as more kits are added.

Select cards on the left — the right panel updates in place.

Pros

  • No caret, no ambiguity, no layout jump — selection & detail are side by side.
  • Detail stays in a fixed spot, so comparing kits is faster (the eye doesn't chase it).
  • Headroom: handles 8–10 kits without the grid pushing the panel off-screen.

Cons / watch-outs

  • Needs horizontal room — the idea form is a single ~720px column, so it's tight.
  • Two focal areas; the empty/initial state of the right panel must be designed.

📱Mobile: collapses to a single column — selected card, then detail directly under it. Effectively becomes Option A, so the two share one responsive end-state.

OPTION C In-place card expansion

The chosen card itself grows to full width and reveals its team & workflows inside the card. Proximity becomes a non-issue because the detail literally is the selected card — the strongest possible signal of “this belongs to your choice.”

Click a card — it expands in place; the others reflow around it.

Pros

  • Unbreakable connection — the preview can't be mistaken for another card.
  • No separate caret element to position or maintain.

Cons / watch-outs

  • Biggest reflow — neighbouring cards jump as the grid re-packs; can feel busy.
  • The expanded card gets tall; on small screens it dominates and others get pushed far down.
  • Grid becomes ragged (one giant cell + small cells).

📱Mobile: works, but the tall expanded card plus a 1-col stack means lots of scrolling to see the other options.

OPTION D Promote & recede (no caret)

Keep the panel below the grid, but drop the misleading caret entirely. On select, the non-chosen cards recede (dim + desaturate) and the chosen one stays lit; the panel header repeats its icon, name and a colour-matched left spine. Connection is carried by similarity & figure/ground, not a pointer — the lowest-risk, smallest-diff fix.

Select a card — others fade back, the panel echoes your pick.

Pros

  • Tiny diff: remove caret, add a dim state + an echoed header. Lowest implementation risk.
  • Focus is unmistakable — only one card is lit, and the panel restates it (not colour-only — icon + label too, so it's WCAG-safe).

Cons / watch-outs

  • Panel still sits below the grid, so there's a little eye-travel from a top-row card.
  • Dimming must stay above 4.5:1 for the receded card text, or drop the text, not just fade it.

Recommendation

⭑ Ship Option A — row-aware inline expansion

It's the most honest fix (the panel is always adjacent to its card and the caret can't lie), the smallest change to today's single-column layout, and it degrades to the exact same thing on mobile. It works unchanged across all three places the picker appears — the new-idea form, onboarding, and the apply-kit dialog.

If we ever widen the idea form / dialog, graduate to Option B (master–detail) — it's the better pattern at scale, and A's mobile layout already is B's mobile layout, so it's a clean upgrade path rather than a rewrite.

Option D is the right call if we want a sub-day fix this week: deleting the caret and dimming the non-selected cards removes the specific “points at the wrong card” bug with minimal code, and A can follow.

Not recommended: Option C — the strongest proximity but the worst reflow and mobile scroll cost; the gain doesn't justify the churn.
